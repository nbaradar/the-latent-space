# Rethinking Meaning, Mind, and the Post-Natural Era

When we ask "will AI be able to think like us" why do we even form the question around human centered thinking? What if a whole new form of thinking or even spirituality emerges? What really is meaning? Humans apply meaning to even the most mundane habits in an effort to counteract the inherit chaos to the universe. Does AI inherently contain meaning?

We are perhaps on the verge of creating the first inorganic life form in history. We are extending life after billions of years of evolution. But is it natural? What are we doing? Whenever humans used social engineering to push an ideology to the extreme (nazis, stalin), it would collapse. Due to the biological nature of human life, we essentially have a reset button. After the collapse, humans come back in the same bodies. But in the 21st century we may have the ability to re-engineer the body down to DNA and create new species and inorganic life forms. What if a social ideology like a new stalin used these new capabilities to create new life with these ideologies in mind? Stalin wanted to create a caste of modest workers who could not use social engineering. A Soviet biologist did attempt to crossbreed humans and apes in the 1920s, no successful human-ape hybrid was ever created. The experiments were led by [Ilya Ivanov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ilya_Ivanov), a specialist in artificial insemination, and were backed by the Soviet government as a way to "prove" Darwin's theory of evolution and undermine religion. 

We live in a unique era where biogenetics/ai is bringing about the post-human era and in some sense the post-natural era. But once we have a life 2.0, won't this retroactively change our view of what life 1.0 is? Because life ends up becoming a transparent combination of biological algorithms. People understand the past through the present but you can't understand the future through the present in the same way.
