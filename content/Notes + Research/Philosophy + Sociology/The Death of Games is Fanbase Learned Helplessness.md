## Dune Awakening: Boiling the Lobster (or Worm) Alive
I want to share my Dune Awakening story and outline how it signals a greater issue in the gaming space. And before I continue, just know I personally was SO excited for this game and LOVED it for the immersion and thought they did an excellent job bringing the Dune universe to life. And I'm just sad about the state of the game today. 

So what is Dune Awakening? It's a game based on the Dune franchise that was MARKETED AS AN MMO. BUT TURNED OUT TO BE A SINGLE PLAYER RPG/CRAFTING/SURVIVAL GAME with multiplayer elements. So what happened? Why would you take away the MMO designation? Well, because the game had no end-game content. 

---
## The Cycle for Players:
### **Game release -> Players are in honeymoon phase**
Players cannot conceive of the design flaws yet, as they are still familiarizing themselves with the systems. The single-player crafting experience is fun in itself, even if the quests and enemy encounters are mediocre. The game has a great feel and immersion.

### **Players start reaching end-game content -> THIS IS WHERE I NOTICED FLAWS**
End game content was trash. There was no incentive loop. There were no reasons to continue playing. There was no real advantage to getting more gear or skills. PvP was almost non-existent. Not enough systems in place to create more incentive for end-game grind. The end-game area known as the "Deep-Dessert" was woefully designed. Basically a huge empty space where players could mine 3 very important resources unique to that area: the spice melange, titanium, and stravidium. 

I start voicing my opinions on reddit, albeit with a little too much sauce (I said people were acting like hostages to the game and need to stop being so sensitive). I truly loved the game (I still log in to pay taxes) and wanted to see it improved. But I was banned from the subreddit after making some dissenting comments and "ruining the vibe"

### **The Lobster gets boiled live -> Players keep coming up with reasons why the game is healthy and alive**
The devs start updating the game to make it easier for players who don't want to deal with PVP in the deep dessert zone. Titanium and Stravidium were moved to the "non combat" zones near the entrance of the deep dessert. Now the only contested resource was the spice. Devs kept pandering to the most lazy players with the most hours. The people who just want to grind a game while watching TV.

Rationalizations are thrown around endlessly in the subreddit
The game has become an identity and a community 
Criticism around the game is perceived negatively as dismissal or cynical take on the game 
"It's only a $60 game"
"They said it's not a typical MMO"
"You've already thrown hundreds of hours into it, you have no right to complain"
"It's not about the end-game, it's about the journey"

### **Most players leave -> Only the most dedicated remain**
Now? The game is practically dead. I see constant subreddit posts of people leaving the game. Announcing it like a eulogy:
"It's been great knowing you guys. May Shai-Hulud provide safe passage into the afterlife and blah blah blah"

Did anyone write an email to the devs before quitting? Did anyone sit down to really analyze the health of the game and how it can be improved for the largest swathe of players? Not just those who spend HOURS building bases. Did the subreddit let other players voice criticism and suggestions without BANNING them from the subreddit?

All this made me realize that thousands of smart adults are just slaves to their own emotions. They don't even realize how much they've tied their ego to this game and what the game means to them. Are they using it to escape? What are they grinding towards? They don't want to step back and really improve it. They just want to defend it. 

Now that those voices were quelled and gone, the game is a husk. No one wants to take responsibility. They just emotionally detach. AND YOU ARE ALL SUCH INTELLIGENT PEOPLE. MUCH MORE-SO THAN ME. I SEE ALL YOUR INSANE MIN-MAXING STRATS. SO WHAT GIVES?

---
## Learned Helplessness
This game died because of learned helplessness, group-think, and a very mismanaged dev team. Which to be fair, how the hell are they supposed to know what the players want? The loudest subset of players are the laziest (in terms of actual game design and coming up with solutions) and don't actually care about the health of the game. Just their own convenience. And they are basically addicted to the game, they are emotionally invested and won't leave. The rest of the players tried to voice their opinions and got punished. The learned helplessness obfuscated what the devs needed to know. Although to be fair, the devs were also hoping pandering to the loudest players would mean hooking in "whales" I'm sure. I hope they learned their lesson.
