Hey! Thanks for stopping by :) 

This is a space where I share projects I'm working on, research I'm doing, useful notes I have, interesting information and insights that I find, and philosophy/sociology/psychology as it relates to technology and AI. 

I'm very intrigued by how [[Artificial General Intelligence (AGI)|AGI]]/[[Artificial Super Intelligence (ASI)|ASI]] (Machine Learning in General, I know AI has become a buzzword) will affect society and humanity on a **macro** and **micro** scale. 

I want to think about/discuss the macro-societal implications of a technology that can come to statistically optimal solutions to a wide array of humanities biggest and smallest problems. If we do not design and evolve these technologies with intention, the disruption they bring could be catastrophic. Economic, Political, Infrastructural, I'm not even sure how free-market capitalism will actually be affected when we are all equipped with our own means of production (through AI assistants). 

I'm also profoundly interested in the micro-individualistic implications. How are LLMs and other ML technologies (Diffusion Models, CNNs, etc) will affect and shape not only our collective conscious but how will it affect each of us individually. Will people come to love their AI companions, fear their AI overlords, or feel indifferent to their AI servants? What does it mean for someone to find meaning in a statistical model? If all of life is a reflection of statistical functions that are constantly taking place around us, is it any different in terms of value than interfacing with an LLM for meaning? 

I haven't really nailed down the structure and how everything will be organized here. I don't really have a plan at the moment. Hoping things will crystalize as I keep maintaining this so it's useful to the public as well. If you have any suggestions let me know.
